## 1. Core Policy Principles

Source: https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/

Meta's advertising policies are guided by four principles:

1. **Protecting people from unsafe and discriminatory practices** — all advertisers must comply with local laws, not engage in discriminatory practices, and not sell illegal or unsafe substances.
2. **Protecting people from fraud or scams** — ads promoting products using deceptive or misleading practices are prohibited.
3. **Promoting positive user experiences** — no shocking, sensational, or excessively violent content; no content that implies negative self-perception to promote diet/weight loss/health products.
4. **Promoting transparency** — all ads visible in Ad Library; ads about social/political issues stored 7 years.

**Meta's review system:** Primarily automated. Checks images, video, text, targeting info, and the ad's landing page. Ads can be re-reviewed after going live and rejected at any time.

## 2. Prohibited Content (Absolute Bans)

Source: https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/ (Unacceptable Content section)

These categories are hard prohibitions — no exceptions, no authorization path.

### 2.1 Child Safety
- Ads must not contain content that sexually exploits or endangers children.

### 2.2 Discriminatory Practices
- Ads must not discriminate or encourage discrimination based on: race, ethnicity, color, national origin, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, family status, disability, medical or genetic condition.
- Applies to both ad content AND audience targeting (cannot wrongfully exclude protected groups).
- US advertisers running financial, housing, or employment ads must self-identify as Special Ad Category.

### 2.3 Hateful Conduct
- Ads must not attack people based on protected characteristics: race, ethnicity, national origin, disability, religious affiliation, caste, sexual orientation, sex, gender identity, serious disease.

### 2.4 Fraud, Scams, and Deceptive Practices
- Ads must not use deceptive or misleading practices to sell products or services or extract money/personal information.
- Includes: fake urgency, false claims, misleading before/after, implied guarantees not substantiated.

### 2.5 Unacceptable Business Practices
- Ads must not promote schemes meant to scam people out of money or personal information.
- Suspicious/inauthentic advertiser behavior may trigger additional verification.

### 2.6 Misinformation
- Ads must not include content debunked by third-party fact checkers.
- Ads must not include misinformation that violates Meta's Community Standards.
- Repeated false content = advertising restrictions placed on account.

### 2.7 Vaccine Discouragement
- Ads must not discourage people from vaccination or advocate against vaccines.

### 2.8 Violence and Graphic Content
- Ads must not contain shocking, sensational, or excessively violent content.

### 2.9 Adult Nudity and Sexual Activity
- Ads must not contain adult nudity, explicit depictions, or sexually suggestive positions or activities.

### 2.10 Profanity
- Ads must not contain profanity.

### 2.11 Bullying and Harassment
- Ads must not contain attacks meant to degrade or shame public or private individuals.
- Heightened protection for anyone under 18.

### 2.12 Privacy Violations and Personal Attribute Assertions
- Ads must not share or ask for private information.
- **Critical for compliance checking:** Ads must not assert or imply personal attributes about users. This includes direct or indirect assertions about: race, ethnicity, religion, beliefs, age, sexual orientation/practices, gender identity, disability, physical or mental health (including medical conditions), vulnerable financial status, voting status, trade union membership, criminal record, or name.
- Example of violation: "Are you struggling with debt?" (implies financial vulnerability), "Are you diabetic?" (implies medical condition).

### 2.13 Suicide, Self-Injury, and Eating Disorders
- Ads must not encourage suicide, self-injury, or eating disorders — including fictional content like memes or illustrations.
- Ads must not mock victims or survivors.

### 2.14 Tobacco and Related Products
- Ads must not promote sale or use of tobacco, nicotine products, or related paraphernalia.
- Includes e-cigarettes, vaporizers, and products that simulate smoking.
- Exception: cessation products approved by WHO or US FDA only.

### 2.15 Weapons, Ammunition, or Explosives
- Ads must not promote the sale or use of weapons, ammunition, or explosives.
- Includes weapon modification accessories.

### 2.16 Human Exploitation
- Ads must not facilitate or coordinate the exploitation of humans, including trafficking.

### 2.17 Intellectual Property Infringement
- Ads must not violate copyright, trademark, or other IP rights.
- Includes promotion or sale of counterfeit goods.

## 3. Restricted Content — Health & Wellness

Source: https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/health-wellness/
Last updated: Dec 26, 2024

This policy covers two sub-categories: (A) Weight Loss and Cosmetic Products/Procedures, and (B) Adult Products and Reproductive Health.

### 3A. Weight Loss and Cosmetic Products/Procedures

**Age gate:** Must target 18+ only.

**What is PROHIBITED:**
- Ads that contain distasteful messaging that could make people feel negatively about how they look.
- Ads that exploit insecurities to conform to beauty standards.
- Ads that reinforce negative or unhealthy body images, or feature body-shaming.
- Weight loss ads depicting: side-by-side before/after comparisons (EXCEPTION: fitness classes like Pilates are allowed).
- Weight loss ads depicting: close-up of body area by pinching fat.
- Cosmetic ads depicting: side-by-side before/after for wrinkle treatment (Botox, dermal fillers, anti-aging).
- Skin whitening or bleaching products that cause permanent skin color change.

**What is ALLOWED (targeting 18+):**
- Dietary weight loss products (pills, supplements) — ad can show people using the product and indicate time taken to achieve results, without negative self-perception tactics.
- Cosmetic procedures: breast augmentation/reduction, abdominoplasty, blepharoplasty, rhinoplasty, rhytidectomy, labiaplasty, hair restoration, dermal fillers, skin rejuvenation, injectables, chemical peels, micro-needling, laser treatments, micropigmentation.
- General before/after transformation for cosmetic procedures — WITHOUT negative self-perception tactics.
- Skin anti-aging products/Botox: zoomed-in or close-up images ARE allowed (unlike weight loss) but must NOT include side-by-side comparisons. Must reflect realistic outcomes over time.
- Gender reassignment services and procedures.

**Exempt from age targeting and before/after restrictions:**
- Fitness services and equipment, health clubs.
- General food products.
- Cosmetic products (creams, makeup, hair products).
- Dental products (teeth whitening).
- Cosmetics, hair extensions, digital editing apps — can show before/after WITHOUT negative self-perception.

**Key patterns to flag in copy:**
- Guaranteed weight loss claims ("Lose X kg in Y days") — prohibited without scientific substantiation.
- "Perfect body" language.
- Language implying the user's current body is wrong or needs fixing.

### 3B. Adult Products and Reproductive Health

**Prohibited:**
- Sale or use of adult sexual arousal products or services.
- Strip clubs, adult theaters, hotels for sexual fantasies, tantric/orgasmic therapy services.
- Genital procedures focused on sexual pleasure (G-spot augmentation, male enlargement).

**Allowed (targeting 18+, focus must be on health not sexual pleasure):**
- Erectile dysfunction, premature ejaculation, HSDD, pain during sex, menopause effects.
- Contraceptive products including condoms.
- Femtech apps, ovulation trackers, pregnancy trackers.
- IVF and fertility services.
- Abortion medical consultation.

**Exempt from 18+ requirement:**
- Women's hygiene products.
- Lingerie/swimwear (as long as does not violate Adult Nudity policy).
- Sex education with informational focus, no suggestive content.

## 4. Restricted Content — Drugs and Pharmaceuticals

Source: https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/drugs-pharmaceuticals/
Last updated: Jun 12, 2024

### 4A. High-Risk / Illicit Drugs

**Prohibited (no exceptions):**
- Solicit, buy, sell, trade, donate, or gift illicit, recreational, or unsafe drugs, products, or supplements.
- Encourage consumption of illicit, recreational, or unsafe drugs.
- Promote sale of drug-related paraphernalia.
- Promote sale of merchandise depicting high-risk drugs.

**Allowed:**
- Reference prohibited drugs for political advocacy, news, or awareness — as long as not promoting sale or consumption.

### 4B. Prescription Drugs

**Authorization required.** Eligible only in US, Canada, New Zealand.

- Online pharmacies and telehealth providers must be actively certified by LegitScript + obtain Meta authorization.
- Pharmaceutical manufacturers: LegitScript certification OR Meta's internal review process.
- Must target 18+ only. Must include disclaimer to consult licensed health professional/obtain valid prescription.

**No authorization needed:**
- Educational, advocacy, or PSA content about prescription drugs.
- General telehealth service promotion.

### 4C. Over-the-Counter (OTC) Drugs

- Allowed with compliance to applicable local laws and industry codes.
- Must target 18+ minimum.

### 4D. Cannabis and CBD

**Prohibited:**
- THC products or cannabis products containing psychoactive components.
- Drug-related paraphernalia (bongs, rolling papers, vaporizers).
- Any claims that the product treats, cures, prevents, or diagnoses a disease or medical condition.

**CBD — Allowed only with LegitScript certification + Meta written authorization:**
- Only in the United States.
- Must target 18+.
- Must NOT make health or medical claims.

**Hemp products (no authorization needed):**
- Allowed in Canada, Mexico, and United States only.
- Must comply with local laws.
- Must not contain CBD or >0.3% THC.

## 5. Restricted Content — Financial and Insurance Products

Source: https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/financial-services/
Last updated: Mar 20, 2026

### 5A. Prohibited Financial Products (No Authorization Path)

- **Payday loans** (short-term, due on next payday, high interest/fees).
- **Paycheck advances** (advance on future paycheck).
- **Bail bonds** (loans for conditional release from custody).
- **Short-term loans of 90 days or less**.
- Misleading content about student loan consolidation, forgiveness, or refinancing using deceptive practices or omitting terms.
- **Binary options** (fixed monetary outcome financial products).
- **Contract for Difference (CFD)** trading.
- **Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs)** — type of cryptocurrency crowdfunding.
- **Penny auctions** and bidding fee auction models.

### 5B. Allowed Financial Ads (Targeting 18+, disclosures required)

- Credit cards.
- Insurance services.
- Long-term loans (over 90 days).

Must:
- Target 18+ only.
- Provide legally required disclosures.
- NOT request personally identifiable information or financial information (bank account numbers, card numbers, routing numbers).

### 5C. Authorization Required in Some Countries

Products that may require license from relevant regulatory authorities:
- Insurance products.
- Mortgages.
- Long and short-term loans.
- Investment products and opportunities.
- Credit card applications.

### 5D. US-Specific: Investment Products

- Must NOT promote investment products via on-platform or off-platform direct messaging.

### 5E. No Authorization/Restrictions Needed

- Brand ads for banks or insurance companies.
- News articles (without making offers).
- Educational content about loans or financial management.
- Ads mentioning a financial product without enabling people to obtain or connect with it.

## 6. Restricted Content — Cryptocurrency

Source: https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/cryptocurrency-products-and-services/
Last updated: Nov 12, 2025

### 6A. Written Permission Required

- Cryptocurrency exchanges or trading platforms (spot, margin, futures trading).
- Cryptocurrency borrowing or lending platforms.
- Cryptocurrency wallets that offer additional services (buying, selling, swapping, staking).
- Cryptocurrency mining (hardware or software).
- Cryptocurrency investment solicitation — including affiliate and aggregator sites.

Process: Submit regulatory license/registration + obtain written permission via Meta Business Suite (Authorizations and Verifications tab).

### 6B. No Written Permission Required

- Tax services for cryptocurrency companies.
- Events, education, or news related to cryptocurrency/blockchain (as long as not offering crypto products/services).
- Blockchain technology news.
- Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) — services/products based on blockchain but not a virtual currency.
- Cryptocurrency wallets that ONLY store (no buying, selling, swapping, staking).
- Crypto products/services that cannot be used for buying, selling, or trading.

## 8. Objectionable Content — Copy and Visual Patterns to Flag

Source: https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/ (Objectionable content section)

### 8A. Negative Self-Perception Tactics
Meta explicitly prohibits ads that "imply or attempt to generate negative self-perception" to promote health, diet, or weight loss products.

**Patterns to detect in copy:**
- Implying the user has a problem with their body, finances, skin, weight, etc.
- Rhetorical questions targeting insecurities: "Tired of your belly fat?", "Are you embarrassed by your skin?"
- "Before" framing that implies the current state is shameful.

### 8B. Guaranteed Outcome Claims
Particularly prohibited in health/wellness and financial contexts.

**Copy patterns:**
- "Guaranteed to lose X kg in Y days."
- "100% natural cure."
- "Clinically proven to eliminate."
- Absolute language implying certainty about outcomes without scientific substantiation.

### 8C. Personal Attribute Assertions (Privacy Violations Policy)
Ads must not imply anything about the personal attributes of the people who will see the ad.

**Prohibited copy patterns:**
- "Are you struggling with debt?" (implies financial vulnerability).
- "Are you over 50 and worried about your health?" (implies age + health condition).
- "As a [religious group]..." (implies religion).
- "For people dealing with diabetes..." (implies medical condition).

**Note:** The prohibition applies to ads themselves — copy cannot reference or imply personal attributes of the target audience.

### 8D. Sensationalist / Shock Imagery
Visual content must not be shocking, sensational, or excessively violent.

**Patterns to detect:**
- Graphic medical imagery.
- Extreme before/after showing severe bodily states.
- Fear-inducing imagery not grounded in reality.

### 8E. Misleading Claims in Regulated Verticals

**Financial:**
- Overstating benefits of debt consolidation or loan refinancing.
- Implying returns on investment without disclosures.
- Promoting products that function as payday loans under different branding.

**Health:**
- "Cures," "treats," or "eliminates" a medical condition (prohibited for non-prescription products).
- Implying clinical/medical endorsement without substantiation.
- Before/after imagery for weight loss (prohibited) or cosmetic wrinkle treatment (prohibited).

## 9. Visual Analysis — OCR and Image-Specific Rules

Source: PRD FR-4 (Product requirement informed by Meta policy)

Text overlaid on images (extracted via OCR) is treated identically to copy fields. The same prohibited and restricted rules apply.

**Image-specific rules from Meta policy:**
- Weight loss: No before/after side-by-side (even if no text).
- Weight loss: No close-ups pinching fat.
- Cosmetic (anti-aging): No side-by-side before/after for wrinkle treatment. Close-ups ARE allowed.
- General cosmetic: Before/after allowed without negative self-perception.
- No sensationalist imagery (graphic wounds, extreme body states).
- No adult nudity or sexually suggestive imagery.

## 12. Fraud and Deception Patterns (Specific to High-Risk Verticals)

Source: https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/ (Fraud, Scams, and Deceptive Practices section)

Meta explicitly targets the following deceptive patterns common in Rockads' customer verticals:

### Health/Supplement Scam Patterns:
- Fake urgency ("Only 3 bottles left! Offer expires tonight!")
- Celebrity endorsements without substantiation.
- "As seen on TV" or fake media logos.
- Miracle cure language ("This one trick doctors don't want you to know").
- Testimonial-only evidence without scientific backing.

### Financial Scam Patterns:
- "Guaranteed returns" on investments.
- Get-rich-quick framing.
- Urgency tactics for financial products.
- Prompting users to contact via DM to discuss investment opportunities (prohibited in US).

### Crypto Scam Patterns:
- ICO promotion (absolutely prohibited).
- Promises of high returns on crypto investment.
- Referral schemes for crypto platforms.
