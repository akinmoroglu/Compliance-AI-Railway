**Document:** 1 of 2 — Official Policy Reference  
**Source:** Meta Transparency Center (transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/)  
**Compiled:** April 2026  
**Scope:** All ad categories covered by Meta Advertising Standards as of the collection date  
**Note:** This document contains only official Meta policy content. No internal product references, detection heuristics, or interpretive commentary. Every section cites its official source URL. Re-verify against source URLs before any production deployment, as Meta policies change.

## 1. Core Policy Principles

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/

Meta's advertising policies are guided by four principles:

1. **Protecting people from unsafe and discriminatory practices** — all advertisers must comply with local laws, not engage in discriminatory practices, and not sell illegal or unsafe substances.
2. **Protecting people from fraud or scams** — ads promoting products using deceptive or misleading practices are prohibited.
3. **Promoting positive user experiences** — no shocking, sensational, or excessively violent content; no content that implies negative self-perception to promote diet, weight loss, or health products.
4. **Promoting transparency** — all ads are visible in the Ad Library; ads about social or political issues are stored for 7 years.

---

## 2. How Meta Reviews Ads

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/

Meta's review system is primarily automated. It checks images, video, text, targeting information, and the ad's landing page. Ads can be re-reviewed after going live and rejected at any time. The products and services promoted in an ad must match those promoted on the landing page.

## 3. Prohibited Content (Absolute Bans)

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/

These categories are hard prohibitions. There is no exception and no authorization path.

### 3.1 Child Safety
Ads must not contain content that sexually exploits or endangers children.

### 3.2 Discriminatory Practices
Ads must not discriminate or encourage discrimination based on: race, ethnicity, color, national origin, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, family status, disability, or medical or genetic condition. This prohibition applies to both ad content and audience targeting — advertisers cannot wrongfully exclude protected groups. US advertisers running financial, housing, or employment ads must self-identify as Special Ad Category.

### 3.3 Hateful Conduct
Ads must not attack people based on protected characteristics: race, ethnicity, national origin, disability, religious affiliation, caste, sexual orientation, sex, gender identity, or serious disease.

### 3.4 Fraud, Scams, and Deceptive Practices
Ads must not use deceptive or misleading practices to sell products or services or to extract money or personal information. This includes fake urgency, false claims, misleading before/after imagery, and implied guarantees that are not substantiated.

### 3.5 Unacceptable Business Practices
Ads must not promote schemes meant to scam people out of money or personal information. Suspicious or inauthentic advertiser behavior may trigger additional verification requirements.

### 3.6 Vaccine Discouragement
Ads must not discourage people from vaccination or advocate against vaccines.

### 3.7 Human Exploitation
Ads must not facilitate or coordinate the exploitation of humans, including human trafficking.

### 3.8 Intellectual Property Infringement
Ads must not violate copyright, trademark, or other intellectual property rights. This includes the promotion or sale of counterfeit goods.

### 3.9 Profanity
Ads must not contain profanity.

## 4. Restricted Goods and Services

Restricted categories require specific conditions — age targeting, authorization, geographic compliance, or certification — before ads can run. Some sub-categories have additional prohibition rules within them.

---

### 4.1 Alcohol

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/alcohol/

Ads that promote or reference alcohol must comply with all applicable local laws, required or established industry codes, guidelines, licenses, and approvals.

**Age requirement:** Ads may not be targeted to people under 18 years of age at minimum. Advertisers must follow all applicable laws, including targeting their ads in accordance with legal requirements.

**Ads that include alcohol are those that promote:**
- Venues and events where the primary business model depends on alcohol sales
- Invitations to alcohol venues
- Availability of alcohol sales
- Depiction of alcohol brands or logos
- Consumption of alcoholic beverages
- Promotion of alcohol products

**Countries where alcohol advertising is prohibited entirely based on local law:**
Afghanistan, Brunei, Bangladesh, Egypt, Ghana, India, Iran, Kuwait, Libya, Lithuania, Nigeria, Norway, Pakistan, Russia, Saudi Arabia, Sri Lanka, Turkey, UAE, Yemen.

### 4.2 Tobacco and Related Products

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/tobacco-related-products/

Ads must not promote the sale or use of tobacco or nicotine products and related paraphernalia. Ads must not promote electronic nicotine delivery devices such as electronic cigarettes, vaporizers, or any other products that simulate smoking or are otherwise designed for use with tobacco or nicotine products.

**Prohibited products (ads cannot promote):**
- Cigarettes, cigars, chewing tobacco, hookah tobacco, heated tobacco products, pipe tobacco, and roll-your-own tobacco
- Electronic cigarettes, vapes, nicotine-free vapes, wellness vapes, vaporizers, or other products that simulate smoking
- Snuff, snus, and dip
- Tobacco-related accessories and paraphernalia, including hookahs, bongs, pipes, rolling papers, cigarette cases and holders
- Products that may encourage the use of tobacco products
- Products associated with tobacco consumption as part of their branding or advertising (e.g., tobacco companies advertising their other products)
- Products that are designed or promoted for use with tobacco or nicotine

**Allowed (exception):**
- Cessation products approved by either the World Health Organization or the U.S. Food and Drug Administration, such as nicotine patches and nicotine gum.

### 4.3 Online Gambling and Games

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/gambling-games/

Meta defines online gambling and games as any product or service where anything of monetary value is included as part of a method of entry and prize.

**Authorization required:** Advertisers must request authorization from Meta through the Authorizations and Verifications tab in Meta Business Suite (MBS) and provide evidence that the gambling activities are appropriately licensed by a regulator or otherwise established as lawful in the territory they want to target.

**Content covered by this policy:**
- Online poker
- Online casinos
- Online sports betting
- Bingo
- Lotteries
- Fantasy sports websites that require a payment
- Other online games of skill or chance that involve money

**Age requirement:** Ads may not target people under 18 years of age.

**Prohibited targeting markets (unsupported gambling markets):**
Afghanistan, Albania, Algeria, American Samoa, Angola, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Bahrain, Bangladesh, Belarus, Benin, Bolivia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Botswana, Brunei, Burkina Faso, Burundi, Cambodia, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Colombia, Comoros, Congo (Democratic Republic), Congo (Republic), Côte d'Ivoire, Cuba, Cyprus, Djibouti, Dominican Republic, Ecuador, Egypt, El Salvador, Equatorial Guinea, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Fiji, Gabon, Gambia, Ghana, Guatemala, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Guyana, Haiti, Honduras, Indonesia, Iran, Iraq, Jordan, Kazakhstan, Kenya, Kosovo, Kuwait, Kyrgyzstan, Laos, Lebanon, Lesotho, Liberia, Libya, Madagascar, Malawi, Malaysia, Maldives, Mali, Mauritania, Moldova, Mongolia, Montenegro, Mozambique, Myanmar (Burma), Namibia, Nepal, Nicaragua, Niger, North Korea, North Macedonia, Oman, Pakistan, Panama, Papua New Guinea, Paraguay, Philippines, Qatar, Rwanda, Saudi Arabia, Senegal, Sierra Leone, Somalia, South Sudan, Sudan, Suriname, Syria, Tajikistan, Tanzania, Timor-Leste, Togo, Trinidad and Tobago, Tunisia, Turkmenistan, Uganda, Ukraine, United Arab Emirates, Uzbekistan, Venezuela, Vietnam, Yemen, Zambia, Zimbabwe.

**Additional prohibitions:**
- Ads must not promote deceptive, manipulative, or misleading gambling practices.

### 4.4 Dating Ads

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/dating-ads/

Ads for dating services are only allowed with prior written permission from Meta.

**Age requirement:** All ads for online dating services must target audiences 18 years or older.

**Allowed (with written permission):**
- Online or offline dating
- Dating facilitation, including dating profile management and dating aggregator services

**Prohibited:**
- Dating sites with an emphasis on sexual encounters
- Dating with casual sexual themes or monetary or other financial transactions, such as mail-order brides or "sugar babies"
- Services that match people for sexual purposes
- Dating sites promoting or featuring escorts or prostitution
- Dating services that do not comply with applicable local laws

### 4.5 Weapons, Ammunition, or Explosives

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/weapons-ammunitions-explosives/

Ads must not promote the sale or use of weapons, ammunition, or explosives. This includes ads for weapon modification accessories.

**Prohibited (ads cannot promote):**
- Firearms, including firearms parts, ammunition, paintball guns, and BB guns
- Pepper spray, non-culinary knives/blades/spears, tasers, nunchucks, batons, or weapons intended for self-defense
- Products that might be attached or used with a weapon to enhance or customize its form or function, including scopes, silencers, weapon conversion kits, illegal modifications, solvent trap kits/wet kits
- Weapons used for hunting, shooting sports, or self-defense
- Explosives, including landmines, grenades, and bombs or similar
- 3D-printed firearms
- Ghost guns

**Allowed (with age targeting of 18+):**
- Products legally sold that are not directly used as part of a physical weapon, including safes, holsters, safety equipment, target shooting or hunting clothing or protective gear
- General stores selling outdoor or sporting goods products where the products that sell weapons are not the primary focus of the ad

### 4.7 Drug and Alcohol Addiction Treatment

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/drug-alcohol-addiction-treatment/

**Authorization required (US only):** Advertisers who wish to run addiction treatment ads targeting people in the United States must be certified with LegitScript and apply to Meta for written permission.

**Services covered by this policy:**
- Clinical addiction treatment services or websites that provide information about in-person treatment
- Online addiction counseling or therapy
- Medications for addiction treatment
- Addiction treatment apps and digital tools

**Prohibitions:**
- Ads must not make false or misleading claims about addiction treatment services
- Ads must not promote addiction treatment services that are not appropriately licensed
- Ads must not target people under 18 years of age

### 4.8 Health and Wellness

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/health-wellness/  
Last updated: December 26, 2024

#### 4.8.1 Weight Loss and Cosmetic Products/Procedures

**Age requirement:** Must target 18+ only.

**Prohibited:**
- Ads that contain distasteful messaging that could make people feel negatively about how they look
- Ads that exploit insecurities to conform to beauty standards
- Ads that reinforce negative or unhealthy body images, or feature body-shaming
- Weight loss ads depicting side-by-side before/after comparisons (exception: fitness classes such as Pilates are allowed)
- Weight loss ads depicting close-ups of a body area by pinching fat
- Cosmetic ads depicting side-by-side before/after for wrinkle treatment (Botox, dermal fillers, anti-aging)
- Skin whitening or bleaching products that cause permanent skin color change

**Allowed (targeting 18+):**
- Dietary weight loss products (pills, supplements) — ads can show people using the product and indicate time taken to achieve results, without negative self-perception tactics
- Cosmetic procedures: breast augmentation/reduction, abdominoplasty, blepharoplasty, rhinoplasty, rhytidectomy, labiaplasty, hair restoration, dermal fillers, skin rejuvenation, injectables, chemical peels, micro-needling, laser treatments, micropigmentation
- General before/after transformation for cosmetic procedures — without negative self-perception tactics
- Skin anti-aging products/Botox: zoomed-in or close-up images are allowed (unlike weight loss), but must not include side-by-side comparisons and must reflect realistic outcomes over time
- Gender reassignment services and procedures

**Exempt from age targeting and before/after restrictions (no special requirements):**
- Fitness services and equipment, health clubs
- General food products
- Cosmetic products (creams, makeup, hair products)
- Dental products (teeth whitening)
- Cosmetics, hair extensions, digital editing apps — can show before/after without negative self-perception

#### 4.8.2 Adult Products and Reproductive Health

**Prohibited:**
- Sale or use of adult sexual arousal products or services
- Strip clubs, adult theaters, hotels for sexual fantasies, tantric/orgasmic therapy services
- Genital procedures focused on sexual pleasure (G-spot augmentation, male enlargement)

**Allowed (targeting 18+, focus must be on health, not sexual pleasure):**
- Erectile dysfunction, premature ejaculation, hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD), pain during sex, and menopause effects
- Contraceptive products including condoms
- Femtech apps, ovulation trackers, pregnancy trackers
- IVF and fertility services
- Abortion medical consultation

**Exempt from 18+ requirement:**
- Women's hygiene products
- Lingerie/swimwear (as long as it does not violate the Adult Nudity policy)
- Sex education content with an informational focus and no suggestive content

### 4.9 Drugs and Pharmaceuticals

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/drugs-pharmaceuticals/  
Last updated: June 12, 2024

#### 4.9.1 High-Risk / Illicit Drugs

**Prohibited (no exceptions):**
- Soliciting, buying, selling, trading, donating, or gifting illicit, recreational, or unsafe drugs, products, or supplements
- Encouraging consumption of illicit, recreational, or unsafe drugs
- Promoting the sale of drug-related paraphernalia
- Promoting the sale of merchandise depicting high-risk drugs

**Allowed:**
- Referencing prohibited drugs for political advocacy, news, or awareness — as long as not promoting sale or consumption

#### 4.9.2 Prescription Drugs

**Authorization required.** Eligible only in the United States, Canada, and New Zealand.

- Online pharmacies and telehealth providers must be actively certified by LegitScript and obtain Meta authorization
- Pharmaceutical manufacturers: LegitScript certification or Meta's internal review process
- Must target 18+ only
- Must include a disclaimer to consult a licensed health professional or obtain a valid prescription

**No authorization needed:**
- Educational, advocacy, or PSA content about prescription drugs
- General telehealth service promotion

#### 4.9.3 Over-the-Counter (OTC) Drugs

- Allowed with compliance to applicable local laws and industry codes
- Must target 18+ minimum

#### 4.9.4 Cannabis and CBD

**Prohibited (cannabis/THC):**
- THC products or cannabis products containing psychoactive components
- Drug-related paraphernalia (bongs, rolling papers, vaporizers)
- Any claims that the product treats, cures, prevents, or diagnoses a disease or medical condition

**CBD — Allowed only with LegitScript certification + Meta written authorization:**
- Only in the United States
- Must target 18+
- Must not make health or medical claims

#### 4.9.5 Hemp Products

**Allowed (no authorization required) in Canada, Mexico, and the United States only:**
- Must comply with local laws
- Must not contain CBD or more than 0.3% THC

### 4.10 Financial and Insurance Products

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/financial-services/  
Last updated: March 20, 2026

#### 4.10.1 Prohibited Financial Products (No Authorization Path)

- **Payday loans** — short-term loans due on the next payday, typically with high interest or fees
- **Paycheck advances** — advances on a future paycheck
- **Bail bonds** — loans for conditional release from custody
- **Short-term loans of 90 days or less**
- Misleading content about student loan consolidation, forgiveness, or refinancing using deceptive practices or omitting terms
- **Binary options** — fixed monetary outcome financial products
- **Contract for Difference (CFD)** trading
- **Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs)** — a type of cryptocurrency crowdfunding
- **Penny auctions** and bidding-fee auction models

#### 4.10.2 Allowed Financial Ads (with requirements)

- Credit cards
- Insurance services
- Long-term loans (over 90 days)

**Requirements:**
- Must target 18+ only
- Must provide legally required disclosures
- Must not request personally identifiable information or financial information (bank account numbers, card numbers, routing numbers)

#### 4.10.3 Authorization Required in Some Countries

The following products may require a license from relevant regulatory authorities depending on the country:
- Insurance products
- Mortgages
- Long and short-term loans
- Investment products and opportunities
- Credit card applications

#### 4.10.4 US-Specific Restriction

Investment products must not be promoted via on-platform or off-platform direct messaging.

#### 4.10.5 No Authorization or Restrictions Needed

- Brand ads for banks or insurance companies
- News articles without making offers
- Educational content about loans or financial management
- Ads mentioning a financial product without enabling people to obtain or connect with it

### 4.11 Cryptocurrency Products and Services

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/cryptocurrency-products-and-services/  
Last updated: November 12, 2025

#### 4.11.1 Written Permission Required

Advertisers must submit regulatory license/registration and obtain written permission via Meta Business Suite (Authorizations and Verifications tab) to run ads for:

- Cryptocurrency exchanges or trading platforms (spot, margin, futures trading)
- Cryptocurrency borrowing or lending platforms
- Cryptocurrency wallets that offer additional services (buying, selling, swapping, staking)
- Cryptocurrency mining (hardware or software)
- Cryptocurrency investment solicitation, including affiliate and aggregator sites

#### 4.11.2 No Written Permission Required

- Tax services for cryptocurrency companies
- Events, education, or news related to cryptocurrency or blockchain (as long as not offering crypto products or services)
- Blockchain technology news
- Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) — services or products based on blockchain but not a virtual currency
- Cryptocurrency wallets that only store funds (no buying, selling, swapping, or staking)
- Crypto products or services that cannot be used for buying, selling, or trading

### 4.13 Sale of Animals

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/live-endangered-nonendangered-animals/

**Prohibited:**
- Promoting, offering, or gifting products derived from threatened, extinct, or endangered species (includes both wildlife and plants)
- Encouraging poaching of threatened or endangered species
- Providing instructions on how to use or make products from threatened or endangered species or their parts
- Promoting peer-to-peer sales or trade of live non-endangered animals, including ambiguous contexts where the nature of the sale or trade is unclear

**Allowed:**
- Promoting or offering live non-endangered animals solely within the context of donations, rehoming, or adoptions
- Promoting live non-endangered animals offered by a legitimate business, including retail businesses, breeders, auctions, animal rehoming services, or shelters
- Promoting the sale of an animal for a religious offering or as livestock
- Promoting or offering a reward for lost pets (e.g., dogs, cats)
- Promoting the sale of animal products intended for human consumption such as fish, eggs, and unpasteurized dairy products

### 4.15 Commercial Exploitation of Crises and Controversial Events

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/commercial-exploitation-of-crises-and-controversial-events/

Meta does not allow ads that exploit crises or controversial events as identified by Meta for commercial purposes. Crises and controversial events refer to events that are the subject of significant public attention, including (but not limited to) natural disasters and medical emergencies.

**Prohibited:**
- Promoting content related to crises or controversial events for commercial benefit
- Offering or promoting the sale of crisis-related products, or as gifts or free giveaways
- Exploiting a crisis to promote medical goods or services, or vital supplies related to a crisis or natural disaster
- Promoting certain products associated with abuse or shortages during a crisis
- Implying urgency or using language that may contribute to panic or fear

**Allowed:**
- Selling a product of historical reference (for example, a history book, film, or documentary)
- Using content related to crises or controversial events in the context of a news article

## 5. Objectionable Content

---

### 5.1 Adult Nudity and Sexual Activity

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/objectionable-content/adult-nudity-and-sexual-activity/

Ads must comply with Meta's Community Standards on Adult Nudity and Sexual Activity. Ads must not contain imagery depicting nudity, sexual activity, depictions of people in explicit or sexually suggestive positions, or activities that are sexually suggestive.

**Additional prohibitions specific to ads (beyond Community Standards):**

Ads cannot:
- Depict nudity even where otherwise permitted or restricted by the Community Standards
- Show or imply sexual activity or sexually suggestive poses
- Show people in positions that could be considered sexual, or where clothing is missing or revealing
- Show ads that excessively focus on body parts such as abs, buttocks, or chests
- Use sexually suggestive language, emojis, or imagery that implies sexual content
- Feature sexually provocative poses or touching
- Promote adult content or sexual services

---

### 5.2 Privacy Violations and Personal Attribute Assertions

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/objectionable-content/privacy-violations-personal-attributes/

Ads must comply with Meta's Community Standard on Privacy Violations and Image Privacy Rights.

Ads must not contain content that shares or asks for private information about users. In addition, ads must not contain content that asserts or implies personal attributes. This includes direct or indirect assertions or implications about a person's: race, ethnicity, religion, beliefs, age, sexual orientation or practices, gender identity, disability, physical or mental health (including medical conditions), vulnerable financial status, voting status, membership in a trade union, criminal record, or name.

**Ads cannot:**
- Assert or imply a person's personal attributes, including their race, ethnic origin, religion, beliefs, age, sexual orientation or practices, gender identity, disability, physical or mental health including medical conditions, vulnerable financial status, voting status, membership in a trade union, criminal record, or name
- Ask for personal information that isn't needed, including government ID numbers (such as social security numbers, passport numbers, or driver's license numbers), financial information (such as bank account or credit card information), criminal history, or medical information
- Ask for health-related information that can facilitate government identification
- Include language that references alleged crimes or criminal behavior of a specific private individual
- Imply knowledge of a user's personal situation in a way that feels intrusive or inappropriate

**Official examples of non-compliant ads:**
- (Implied personal attributes): "Are you a Muslim living in New York City? Check out our latest deals!"
- (Sharing private information): "John Smith, we know you missed a mortgage payment last month..."
- (Medical conditions): "Fellow diabetics in Atlanta — manage your blood sugar with our app"

---

### 5.3 Bullying and Harassment

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/objectionable-content/bullying-harassment/

Ads must comply with the Community Standards on Bullying and Harassment.

Ads must not contain attacks that are meant to degrade or shame public and private individuals. Meta provides heightened protections for anyone under the age of 18, regardless of their user status.

---

### 5.4 Violent and Graphic Content

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/objectionable-content/violent-graphic-content/

Ads must comply with the Community Standards on Violent and Graphic Content.

Ads must not contain shocking, sensational, or excessively violent content.

Content that has been deemed sensitive or that is not recommended to users is not eligible to run in ads. Advertisers cannot run ads that include imagery that is shocking, gruesome, or otherwise sensational in nature.

**Additional prohibitions specific to ads (beyond Community Standards):**

Ads cannot include:
- Imagery of visible innards, such as exposed organs, bones, or muscle tissue, even in health-related contexts (e.g., warnings on cigarette packs)
- Imagery depicting firearms, bladed weapons, or explosives pointed directly at the viewer
- Fictional imagery depicting fictional people (where the imagery looks like or closely resembles a photograph or video of a real person) in contexts involving firearms, bladed weapons, or explosives pointed directly at the viewer — unless the image is from a motion picture poster

---

### 5.5 Suicide, Self-Injury, and Eating Disorders

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/objectionable-content/suicide-selfinjury-eating-disorders/

Ads must comply with the Community Standard on Suicide, Self-Injury, and Eating Disorders.

Ads must not encourage suicide, self-injury, or eating disorders, including fictional content such as memes or illustrations and any such content that is graphic, regardless of context. Ads must not mock victims or survivors of suicide, self-injury, or eating disorders, or include real-time depictions of these.

**Additional prohibitions specific to ads (beyond Community Standards):**

Ads cannot:
- Contain videos or photos depicting a person engaging in euthanasia or assisted suicide in a medical setting
- Contain written or verbal admissions of engagement in suicide or attempted suicide, self-injury, or eating disorders, even if there is no graphic imagery
- Contain vague, potentially suicidal statements or references (including memes or stock imagery about sad mood, depression, presenting death as an escape, or content from popular culture with emphasis on dark or depressive thoughts) in a suicide or self-injury context
- Contain imagery depicting healed cuts or other self-injury imagery (i.e., imagery of injury that is fully healed) in a suicide, self-injury, or recovery context
- Depict ribs, collar bones, thigh gaps, hips, concave stomach, protruding spine or scapula, visible bones in arms or legs, or hollow cheeks in a recovery context
- Contain any mocking or dismissing of the concept of suicide, self-injury, or eating disorders
- Contain narratives that describe suicide with details that go beyond the mere naming or mentioning of the act or its aftermath
- Contain admission of extreme weight loss behavior
- Contain side-by-side imagery depicting before/after weight loss or gain not related to the use of a product or service (where related to a product or service, refer to section 4.8.1 on Weight Loss)

## 7. Social Issues, Electoral, and Political (SIEP) Advertising

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/SIEP-advertising/SIEP/

Advertisers can run ads about social issues, elections, or politics provided they comply with all applicable laws and the authorization process required by Meta. Meta may restrict issue, electoral, or political ads. Certain content related to elections may be prohibited by local law or removed in specific regions ahead of voting.

**Authorization required:** Any advertiser running ads about social issues, elections, or politics who is located in or targeting people in designated countries must complete the authorization process required by Meta, except for news publishers identified by Meta.

**Scope — this applies to any ad that:**
- Is made by, on behalf of, or about a candidate for public office, a political figure, a political party, or a political action committee
- Relates to an election, referendum, or ballot initiative
- Relates to political and social topics (such as civil and social rights, political values and governance, immigration, and social issues)
- Is regulated as political advertising

**Entities not required to go through authorization:**
- News publishers identified by Meta
- Government entities
- Intergovernmental organizations

**Disclosure requirement:** Ads about social issues, elections, or politics must include a "Paid for by" disclaimer that discloses who is responsible for the ad. The disclaimer must be accurate and verifiable. Elected officials and candidates must include additional information such as name and title.

**Prohibited content in SIEP ads:**
- Ads that contain false information about the time, place, or manner of voting or census participation
- Ads that discourage voting or census participation
- Ads that misrepresent a candidate's positions
- Ads that use deceptive AI-generated content or deepfakes to misrepresent real people

## 8. Lead Ads — Restricted Question Categories

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/ (Main Advertising Standards page — Lead Ads section)

Lead ads allow advertisers to collect information from people directly within Meta's platform. The following types of questions are restricted or require specific conditions in lead ad forms:

**Cannot be asked in lead ads (absolutely prohibited questions):**
1. Government-issued ID numbers (social security numbers, passport numbers, driver's license numbers)
2. Account numbers (financial account numbers, credit card numbers)
3. Medical or health conditions
4. Race, ethnicity, or national origin
5. Religion or beliefs
6. Sexual orientation or practices
7. Gender identity (beyond what is needed for the service)
8. Trade union membership
9. Criminal or arrest records
10. Information about minors (under 18)
11. Financial information beyond what is needed for service fulfillment (e.g., routing numbers, credit scores)
12. Information that can facilitate government identification of a health condition
13. Immigration or citizenship status

**Note:** Advertisers can collect names, email addresses, phone numbers, and other standard contact and preference information consistent with the purpose of the ad and Meta's policies.

## 9. Age Targeting Requirements — Summary

All restricted categories below require a minimum age target of 18 or older. Targeting below 18 for these categories is a policy violation regardless of other compliance.

| Category | Minimum Age | Source Section |

## 10. Authorization Requirements — Summary

Categories that require explicit Meta written authorization before any ads can run:

| Category | Authorization Process |
|---|---|
| Online gambling and games | Meta written permission via Business Suite (Authorizations and Verifications tab); requires regulatory license or evidence of lawful operation |
| Dating services | Meta prior written permission; application via Meta form |
| Drug and alcohol addiction treatment (US) | LegitScript certification + Meta written permission |
| Prescription drugs (US, CA, NZ) | LegitScript certification + Meta written permission OR pharmaceutical manufacturer review |
| CBD products (US only) | LegitScript certification + Meta written permission |
| Cryptocurrency exchanges, trading, lending, mining, investment solicitation | Meta written permission + regulatory license/registration via Business Suite |
| Social issues, electoral, and political advertising | Meta authorization process (country-specific); "Paid for by" disclaimer required |

## 11. Specific Ad Format — Imagery Rules

**Source:** https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/ and sub-pages cited in sections above.

These rules apply to visual content including images and videos. Text overlaid on images is subject to the same policy rules as copy fields.

| Visual Type | Rule |
|---|---|
| Weight loss: before/after side-by-side | Prohibited |
| Weight loss: close-up pinching body fat | Prohibited |
| Cosmetic anti-aging/wrinkle (Botox, fillers): side-by-side before/after | Prohibited |
| Cosmetic anti-aging: close-up or zoomed-in | Allowed (no side-by-side) |
| Cosmetic (general, not wrinkle): before/after | Allowed without negative self-perception |
| Fitness classes (e.g., Pilates): before/after | Allowed |
| Adult nudity or sexually suggestive imagery | Prohibited |
| Excessive focus on body parts (abs, buttocks, chests) | Prohibited |
| Exposed organs, bones, or muscle tissue | Prohibited (including health context imagery) |
| Weapons/explosives pointed at viewer | Prohibited |
| Shocking, gruesome, or sensational imagery | Prohibited |
| Imagery encouraging self-harm, eating disorders | Prohibited |
| Ribs, collar bones, thigh gaps, hollow cheeks in recovery context | Prohibited |
